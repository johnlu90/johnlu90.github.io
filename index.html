The Science Behind Ripple

Two books. One breakthrough.

Ripple is built on the most compelling findings from two landmark books — on how children actually learn faster, and what truly motivates them to keep going.

📘
Make It Stick: The Science of Successful Learning
Brown, Roediger & McDaniel — Harvard University Press, 2014

"Trying to recall information from memory is far more effective for long-term learning than re-reading or re-studying it."

What this means for your child: Ripple's home activities are built as recall challenges — not re-reads. Every night your child retrieves and applies what they learned, which multiplies how much they actually retain.

"Learning that feels easy is shallow and fades fast. It's precisely the struggle that makes knowledge stick."

What this means for your child: Ripple calibrates each activity to be slightly challenging — never impossible, never too easy. The productive struggle is the point. That's how durable learning is built.

"Spacing practice over time — returning to material days later — produces dramatically stronger long-term retention than massed practice."

What this means for your child: Ripple's Monday dashboard flags which concepts haven't been revisited in 7+ days and re-activates them before they fade. Your child isn't just learning — they're retaining.

🧠
10 to 25: The Science of Motivating Young People
David Yeager — Simon & Schuster, 2024

"Young people thrive when adults hold high standards and provide high support simultaneously — this is the Mentor Mindset."

What this means for your child: Every Ripple interaction is designed around this principle. It's never just hard, and never just easy. It says: "This matters, and I believe you can do it."

"Students persist longer and perform better when they feel that their struggles are normal, expected, and part of growing — not signs of failure."

What this means for your child: Ripple's confidence tap is private, warm, and non-comparative. It tells children: your feelings about this are valid, and your teacher cares about them.

"When young people see their learning as connected to something beyond grades — to helping their family, to becoming who they want to be — engagement skyrockets."

What this means for your child: Ripple positions the student as the "expert teacher" at home. They're not just practicing math — they're helping their parent understand something. That identity shift is powerful.

A product that gets both right is qualitatively different from anything in the market today.

Make It Stick answers: given a motivated student, how should practice be structured? 10 to 25 answers: how do we create the conditions where a student wants to engage in the first place? Ripple is the first product built on both — simultaneously, every single day.
