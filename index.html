The Origin Story

October 14th — A rainy Tuesday

She appeared at the door like a small ghost — white as fresh snow, trembling slightly, and looking at us as though she had always known we would open it.

The Night She Chose Us

It was a cold October evening when we first heard the tiny, insistent sound at the front door. Not a desperate cry — more like a polite, firm announcement. When we opened it, there she was: a perfectly white cat, no larger than a loaf of bread, sitting square in the center of the welcome mat as though she had always owned it.

She had no collar. No chip. Just enormous blue-grey eyes that looked straight through us and a small golden bell tied around her neck with a fraying red ribbon — as if someone, somewhere, had already named her something beautiful and then let her go off to find her own home.

We put out a bowl of water. She drank, walked past us into the living room, circled the warmest spot on the rug, and simply sat down. Not with the hesitance of a stray — but with the casual authority of someone who had finally arrived somewhere they had already planned to be.

We tried, briefly, to find her previous family. We posted. We asked around. Nobody came forward. Which is how we came to understand what Luna had understood all along: she hadn't been abandoned — she had simply been searching. And now she was done.

A Day in the Life

Luna's Daily Schedule

06:00 — 07:30 🌅 The Morning Audit

Luna wakes before sunrise and conducts a thorough inspection of every window — checking that the neighbourhood is still in order and no birds have become too comfortable on her sill.

07:30 — 08:00 🍽️ Breakfast Supervision

She sits beside her food bowl precisely four minutes before breakfast is served, projecting focused telepathic disappointment until someone takes the hint.

08:00 — 12:00 ☀️ The Long Nap (Phase I)

Curled on the windowsill in the first patch of morning sunlight. Position adjusted every 22 minutes as the light moves. She treats this with great seriousness.

12:00 — 13:00 🎭 The Zoomies

An explosive, apparently unprovoked sprint through the entire house. Origin and destination: unknown. Duration: exactly as long as it takes to knock something off a shelf.

13:00 — 16:00 📚 The Long Nap (Phase II)

This time: on someone's laptop, an open book, or any piece of paper that looks important. Her gravity at this hour is extraordinary and impossible to disturb.

16:00 — 18:00 🐦 Bird TV

Stationed at the back window, chattering softly at pigeons in a language only she understands. Occasionally turns to look at us as if requesting a second opinion.

18:00 — 21:00 🤝 Social Hour

Luna becomes briefly, magnanimously affectionate — draping herself over knees, headbutting elbows, and purring at a frequency that seems to vibrate the whole sofa.

21:00 — 06:00 🌙 Night Watch

She patrols the dark house at 3am, occasionally knocking one (1) item from the kitchen counter to confirm the laws of physics remain in effect. Then sleeps at the foot of the bed.
